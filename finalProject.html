FINAL PROJECT

The Final Project will be a fully-developed work of sound art. Explore the physical presentation of sound.

This should be an installation. It should be a piece that you can install and let run.

You must carefully consider the formal elements of your piece: you are making a sculpture. The wires, speakers, playback devices, plugs; all of these things are part of your sculpture and should be handled carefully.

All sorts of extra-sonic material may be a part of your piece: drawings, photos, video monitors, projectors, glass cubes, elephants, waterfalls, etc.

Your piece should be one which can be installed in a gallery space, or in our classroom. If you wish to make a site-specific piece for someplace like a stairwell you must contact Toni Whittle-Ciprazo in the art office and win her over to your idea.

The length of your piece should be either indeterminate or very, very long. You must give up control over what people hear, and how they hear it.

Whatever you make, it needs to be something which a person can experience without your help: without you explaining anything, pushing any buttons, etc.

Since the parameters for this project are so broad, be sure to talk to me soon and often so we can establish a productive dialogue and we can help each other stay on the same page with what it is you hope to achieve!

Hopefully, this project will be the culmination of all of your hopes and dreams as they concern sound art! I'm looking forward to experiencing your work!
